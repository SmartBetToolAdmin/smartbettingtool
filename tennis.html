Tennis

Introduction to Tennis

Tennis is a dynamic and skillful sport played on a rectangular court divided by a net. It is a popular individual and doubles sport that tests agility, precision, and strategy. Matches are played on various surfaces like grass, clay, and hard courts, adding unique challenges.

How the Sport is Played

The game involves hitting a ball over the net into the opponent’s court. Points are scored when the opponent fails to return the ball within the boundaries or commits a fault. Players use rackets to strike the ball and aim to win sets to secure victory in the match.

Rules of Tennis

Key rules include:

  • Matches are scored in sets, with the first to win six games typically winning the set.
  • A player must win by at least two points in a tiebreak to secure the set.
  • Faults occur when a serve lands outside the designated service box or fails to clear the net.

Game Styles

Players often adopt styles such as aggressive baseline play, serve-and-volley, or counter-punching, depending on their strengths and the surface being played on.

Strategies

Effective strategies include:

  • Exploiting the opponent’s weaknesses and varying shot placement.
  • Using spin and pace to control rallies and dictate play.
  • Maintaining fitness and focus to endure long matches.

How to Win

Winning requires excellent shot-making, strategic play, and mental toughness. Players must adapt to different conditions and outwit their opponents to emerge victorious.
